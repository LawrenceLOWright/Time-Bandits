As you walk into the Kalèfe, the smell of freshly ground beans and artisanal pastries fills the air. At a reclaimed wood table, a man with a grand mustache savors his meticulously crafted 10$ coffee while flipping through a limited-edition copy of Catcher in the Rye.
You study the paintings on the wall. That one seems to be a blob. Oh, and that one seems to be… another blob. You’re kind of catching a pattern here.
“Greg? Yeah, I totally broke up with him last week,” one barista says. The other offers her a high five. “It’s about time,” the other one says. 
Mustering your courage, you go up and order a croissant. It’s 12 dollars - yeesh - but you have enough in your wallet to cover it. It’s delicious.
